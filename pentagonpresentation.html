The Badge

What Was "Citizenship in Society"?

Introduced after 2020, the Citizenship in Society merit badge was required for Eagle Scout rank. It was one of 13 mandatory badges you needed to earn the highest honor in Scouting.

The badge asked Scouts to think critically about the world around them — to understand how discrimination works, how to be a leader who includes everyone, and how to stand up for people who are treated unfairly. It explored concepts like bias, inequality, and what it really means to be a citizen in a diverse country.

In short: it was about turning Scouts into thoughtful, aware, courageous leaders — not just in the wilderness, but in society. Isn't that kind of the whole point?

The Timeline

How It Went Down

2024 — Scouting America Rebrands
The Boy Scouts of America officially renamed itself Scouting America, reflecting its growing membership of girls and its inclusive mission. Hegseth was furious.
Early 2025 — Pentagon Threatens to Pull Support
Under Hegseth, the Pentagon began reviewing its century-old partnership with Scouting, threatening to kick Scout troops off military bases and cut Jamboree logistics support — worth millions — unless the org complied.
January 2026 — Scouting Sends a Concession Letter
Scared of losing Pentagon support, Scouting America's leadership sent Hegseth a letter proposing to drop the Citizenship in Society badge, add a Military Service badge, and dissolve their DEI committee.
February 27, 2026 — The Badge is Killed
Citizenship in Society officially discontinued. Scouts can no longer start its requirements. A new Military Service merit badge is introduced as its replacement path to Eagle.
NOW — 6-Month Clock is Ticking
Hegseth placed Scouting "on notice." The Pentagon will review compliance in six months and threatens to sever all support if they're unsatisfied. The pressure is ongoing.
