⚡ Top 10 Exam Strategies

  • MCQ: Read the stimulus first — identify data type (graph/table/diagram) before reading the question.
  • Eliminate: "Always/never" answers are almost always wrong. Pick the most complete, accurate option.
  • Don't confuse correlation with causation in data questions — the AP exam loves this trap.
  • FRQ: Read ALL parts before writing. Plan so you don't repeat yourself or miss a sub-part.
  • Define every term you use. Write "enzyme (a biological catalyst)" not just "enzyme."
  • "Describe" = WHAT. "Explain" = HOW and WHY (mechanism). These earn different points.
  • Hardy-Weinberg: memorize p+q=1 AND p²+2pq+q²=1. Know what each symbol represents. Show all work.
  • Chi-square: χ²=Σ[(O−E)²/E]. df = categories − 1. p < 0.05 = significant, reject null.
  • Experimental design FRQ: state IV, DV, control group, controlled variables, and measurement method.
  • Know exactly where ATP, NADH, FADH₂ are produced: glycolysis, Krebs cycle, ETC.
