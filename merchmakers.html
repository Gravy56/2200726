THE PROCESS
01
Set Your Canvas
Open Canva (or Slides). Set page to a comic ratio — 6.625" × 10.25" is standard. Or just use a square. Your call.
5 MIN
02
Build Your First Panel
Draw a rectangle. Give it a thick border. Drop in your first character asset. Position them. Add a caption or speech bubble.
10–15 MIN
03
Flow Through the Storyboard
Use last session's storyboard as your script. Panel by panel, moment by moment. Don't skip ahead — build in sequence.
MAIN BLOCK
04
Text & Lettering
Add dialogue. Keep it SHORT in each bubble — comics breathe. Bold for emphasis. Comic Sans is actually fine here (seriously).
ONGOING
05
Review & Refine
Print preview or full-screen it. Does the eye flow naturally? Are the panels telling the story? Fix what feels stuck.
END OF SESSION
TIPS FROM THE PROS
★ Vary Your Panel Sizes
Big panel = big moment. Small panel = quick beat. Use size to control pacing — the reader feels it even if they don't know why.
★ Leave Breathing Room
Don't cram every panel. Whitespace is part of the art. Gutters matter. Silence between panels is as powerful as sound.
★ Stay Consistent
Consistency is more important than perfection. Pick your character's angle and stick to it. Readers trust consistency.
★ Less Text, More Image
If you wrote 3 sentences of dialogue, cut to 1. The picture carries 80% of the story. Trust what you drew.
★ Sound Effects Are Visual
POW. CRASH. WHOOSH. Make them big. Make them part of the panel design, not an afterthought tossed on top.
★ End on a Question
The last panel should make someone want to flip the page — even if there's no next page yet. That's the hook.
